## The financial drag

Purchase price accounting benefits from fair-value adjustments were running at C$117 million per quarter in late 2025, declining to roughly C$80 million and largely running off by Q2 2026. RBC built new capabilities to serve former HSBC clients — foreign currency accounts and certain money-movement features — that weren't previously in RBC's product set and are now being cross-sold to the broader customer base. That's a genuine strategic gain. The HSBC acquisition also gave RBC a stronger foothold with internationally-connected clients, particularly in Vancouver's Chinese-Canadian community, and a deeper commercial banking pipeline.

The deal isn't a disaster — RBC integrated quickly, the client book is high quality, and cost synergies are being realized. The Big Six treat fintech competition as a risk factor disclosure item, not a strategic priority. It's treated as one of 30+ generic risks rather than the specific structural challenge it is.
